Providing an opportunity for rangatahi to engage with their respective iwi leaders within RIAHC and participate alongside them as representatives of their iwi. Tuakana – Teina mentoring.

An opportunity for rangatahi to be exposed to an environment where issues of fundamental importance to our iwi are addressed.

The appointment as a Rangatahi Intern is for an initial term of 12 months. However any intern is eligible to serve a further three year term, subject to re-nomination by the board at the end of their 12 months term and in accordance with our Rangatahi Intern Programme.

Time: Interns must have the time to give to their board work. An effective board relies on members being able to give priority to preparation and attendance. It is anticipated that the Rongowhakaata Iwi Asset Holding Company (RIAHC) will meet 10 to 12 times per year, depending upon the nature and scope of activities undertaken by the Rongowhataata Iwi Trust (RIT).

Rangatahi Intern Attributes

It is desirable that the group’s combined skills and experience includes a good understanding of:

  • The relevant aspects of the settlement, The Company act, the Finance act.
  • Maori political structures, including governance and the relative importance and functions of iwi, hapu and whanau.
  • The operational parameters of trusts and the responsibilities and liabilities of trustees.

Rangatahi Intern Qualities

In addition to this criteria potential candidates must show personal qualities where they are able to work alongside others to get to an outcome. These may include skills in:

  • Clarity of debate,
  • Integrity and courage,
  • Professionalism in conduct,
  • Commitment to the vision of the organization.
